sweet macarons

Description

How to make macarons not macaroons

Ingredients

  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 1 1/4 cups powdered sugar
  • 3 egg whites
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • Food coloring (optional)
  • Buttercream, ganache, or jam for filling
  • Piping bag with a round tip
  • Parchment paper

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 300°F (150°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Sift the almond flour and powdered sugar together into a large bowl, ensuring there are no lumps.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat the egg whites until they form soft peaks.
  4. Gradually add the granulated sugar to the egg whites while continuing to beat, achieving stiff peaks.
  5. Gently fold the sifted dry ingredients into the egg whites in three parts, using a spatula, to avoid deflating the mixture.
  6. If desired, add a few drops of food coloring and mix gently.
  7. Transfer the macaron batter to a piping bag with a round tip.
  8. Pipe 1 1/2-inch circles onto the parchment-lined baking sheet, spaced about 2 inches apart.
  9. Tap the baking sheet on the counter several times to release any air bubbles.
  10. Allow the piped macarons to rest for 30 minutes to form a skin, which is crucial for the formation of "feet" during baking.
  11. Bake the macarons in the preheated oven for 15-18 minutes, or until they are set and have formed feet.
  12. Allow the macarons to cool completely on the baking sheet before removing.
  13. Once cooled, fill the macarons with buttercream, ganache, or jam, and sandwich them together.
